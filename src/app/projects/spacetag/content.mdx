SpaceTag is an award-winning 3D satellite tracking web application focusing on
simplicity and ease of use, featuring an extensive list of satellites to choose
from and manage through the user-friendly control center.

<Image alt="SpaceTag's homepage." src={spacetagHomePage} />

## Features

- List of over {new Intl.NumberFormat("en").format(8000)} satellites
- Custom objects via
  [Two-Line Element](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two-line_element_set) format
- Past, present and future satellite positions on a chosen date and at a chosen
  time
- Support for gestures on touch devices
- Refresh-proof, persistent settings
- Satellite orbit trajectories

## My role

I was part of
[500 International Space Station](https://2022.spaceappschallenge.org/challenges/2022-challenges/track-the-iss/teams/500-international-space-station)
— a team of four [Rzeszów University of Technology](https://w.prz.edu.pl/en)
Computer Engineering majors and
[Student Research Group of Computer Science - ‘Code’](https://kod.prz.edu.pl/)
members, who developed this project during
[2022 NASA International Space Apps Challenge](https://2022.spaceappschallenge.org/)
hackathon. It was my first time taking part in a competition of this kind.

Since my teammates specialised in backend development and the application was
frontend-oriented, I had many responsibilities regarding the technical side of
the project:

- Initialising the project
- Making architectural and technological decisions
- Building user interfaces
- Setting up global state management
- Implementing application's internal logic
- Deploying the application

The success of the project by far exceeded our expectations. Our team took third
place and multiple awards, including a trip to [SES](https://ses.com/) (one of
the world's leading satellite owners and operators) headquarters in Betzdorf,
Luxembourg.

We saw the opportunity for our junior colleagues to learn a lot by contributing
to this project and decided to continue the development of the project under
[Student Research Group of Computer Science - ‘Code’](https://kod.prz.edu.pl/).
I was appointed as a new project leader as other teammates took on other
projects. As a project leader I inherited several new duties:

- Making the code more modular and extensible
- Optimising the application in terms of performance
- Designing the logo
- Planning and holding regular team meetings
- Overseeing project progress
- Assigning tasks
- Conducting code reviews
- Peer mentoring
